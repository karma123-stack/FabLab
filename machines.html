3d printer

An FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) 3D printer builds objects layer by layer by extruding melted thermoplastic filament through a heated nozzle. Known for their affordability and ease of use, FDM printers work with various materials like PLA, ABS, and PETG. Ideal for prototyping.

Sweing Machine

An electric sewing machine is a sewing device powered by an electric motor, which drives the needle and fabric feed mechanism. It allows for faster and more precise stitching compared to manual machines.

Laser Cutting

A laser cutter is like a super-powered pair of scissors. Instead of using metal blades, it uses a strong, focused beam of light (called a laser) to cut through different materials. Imagine it like a lightsaber from the movies, but it's used to make cool shapes and designs out of things like wood, metal, and plastic.

Computerized Numerical Control(CNC)

CNC (Computer Numerical Control) machines are automated manufacturing tools controlled by a computer program. They use pre-programmed instructions to precisely cut, shape, or engrave materials like metal, wood, and plastic.

Carpentary

Carpentry is the skilled trade of working with wood to build, install, and repair structures, furniture, and other wooden objects. Carpenters use tools like saws, hammers, chisels, and drills to cut, shape, and join wood.
